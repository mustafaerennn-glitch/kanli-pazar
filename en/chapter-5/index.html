Chapter 5

What Was Said After Kanlı Pazar

pp. 239–260
📎 17 February 1969, Babıali'de Sabah
Added April 2026

What was said in the immediate aftermath of Kanlı Pazar, while the events were still fresh, bears the marks of the polarization between right and left. The words and statements are like the stones that were thrown. The right openly defended the Sixth Fleet. In the right's view, telling the Sixth Fleet to leave meant handing the country over into Russia's arms. Those who said no to the Sixth Fleet were "lackeys of the Muscovite." For this reason, the patriotic people had put the reds in their place and spoiled their game. What happened in Taksim was "the uprising of the communists, the fury of the patriots." Statements from the period's governor show that, at the level of the state too, those who organized the rally were seen as the guilty party. The organizers were provocateurs, and it was they who caused the events to break out. In these statements, there is no trace whatsoever of any acknowledgment that the attack was organized, that clubs and ribbons had been handed out, or that there had been cooperation with the police. The statements of leftists, meanwhile, bear the marks of the divisions among themselves. The faction of leftists close to TİP, described as "socialist revolutionaries," characterized their attackers as deceived Muslims, and drew attention as well to the junta-minded elements among the rally's participants; the MDD-ists [National Democratic Revolutionaries], for their part, stated that they saw the attack as a fight between those waging a war of independence and those who had sold out to imperialism. The common thread in the leftists' statements is that they had been caught unprepared for the attack. They stated that, from then on, they would exercise their right to self-defense. Intellectuals, as can be seen in Abdi İpekçi's case in particular, drew attention to the "government's" hand in the attack. Although the leftists even supplied the license plates of the vehicles used in the attack, no investigation would be carried out on this matter; Minister of the Interior Faruk Sükan would instead announce the names of 48 people found guilty as a result of the investigation carried out. Yet every one of these 48 people was a leftist. In the aftermath of the events, two people seen holding knives in newspaper photographs were arrested, and a case was opened against 4 people, including these two detainees. According to the state, these 4 people were the sole culprits of Kanlı Pazar.

Kanlı Pazar Pamphlet
About This Document

This two-page pamphlet, dated 27 February 1969 and held in the TÜSTAV archive, was prepared and distributed immediately after Kanlı Pazar. The pamphlet combines photographs of the attack, images from the march, police cordons, and various frames from the aftermath of the event, and includes the views of DİSK Chairman Kemal Türkler and Deputy Chairman Ehliiman Tuncer.

The pamphlet's headline — "The American Sixth Fleet Has Done Its Job Once Again, Setting Its America-Loving Tools Upon the Patriots" — reads like a summary of how the massacre was understood. The opposition was framed as one between "patriots" and "America-lovers," with the government of the day characterized as a mere "tool." The pamphlet also names these "America-lovers" directly; beneath a photograph showing attackers, together with police, beating a demonstrator they had caught among them, the following words appear: "What is national, what is Turkish, what is student, what is unity about these men."

The document does not merely offer an assessment of Kanlı Pazar; it also shows how the period's youth — the "socialist revolutionary" leftist youth in particular — framed the events, which slogans they foregrounded, and what kind of political narrative they were trying to construct in the public eye.

SADUN AREN (continuing) — No, sir. Their definition of communism covers everyone except themselves.

SADUN AREN (continuing) — The Istanbul events, the recent Istanbul events, the bloody Istanbul events, are clear proof of this.

TAHİR YÜCETÜRK (Adana) — Were you there too?

SADUN AREN (continuing) — I was at that march, I took part in that march.

TAHİR YÜCETÜRK (Adana) — You too, as part of the organization.

ON BEHALF OF THE T. İ. P. GROUP, SADUN AREN (continuing) — This was a march for which legal permission had been obtained, and I too took part in it, and it reached Taksim Square; I am relating to you what I saw. The march entered Taksim Square in formation.

TAHİR YÜCETÜRK (Adana) — You too, included..

SADUN AREN (continuing) — Yes. A moment later, a moment came when, after around a thousand people had entered Taksim Square from Gümüşsuyu, the Riot Police began throwing bombs — noise bombs, ones that make a loud sound. And there was panic; from Gümüşsuyu, together with the reactionary forces and attackers behind the police, in the marching column and toward Gümüşsuyu.

OSMAN SARAÇ (Tokat) — That was the Turkish Nation.

CHAIRMAN — Mr. Saraç; please, sir, do not interrupt. Friends, please, we are in deliberation. Mr. Saraç, please, sir, do not interrupt, you may respond when you take the floor.

OSMAN SARAÇ (Tokat) — Given the chance, I would respond a great deal.

CHAIRMAN — Give your material to your Group Deputy Chairman.

ON BEHALF OF THE T. İ. P. GROUP, SADUN AREN (continuing) — A small part of the marching column, around a thousand people, was trapped in a pincer, and the Riot Police played the primary role in this trapping. I saw this with my own eyes. Had they thrown the bombs at the reactionaries when they attacked, the reactionaries would have scattered — but they did not do this; they threw them toward Gümüşsuyu instead, and closed off that area, preventing more of the marching column from entering, and I was able to see the other events that unfolded only through photographs, just as you were, because I could only see the spot where I myself was standing. This is what happened at the spot where I was.

NAZMİ ÖZOĞUL (Edirne) — Did you prepare the Molotov cocktails yourselves?

SADUN AREN (continuing) — And here, of course, the managers of domestic politics — the Minister of the Interior above all — bear great responsibility, sole responsibility. And the Governor of Istanbul was, 4–5 years earlier, the Governor of Bursa at the time of a similar event that took place in Bursa — he is quite experienced in these matters. Now, an investigation is being carried out into these matters. Of course the truth will come to light. Our country, thank God, is a country developed enough for this. Nothing can be covered up. But as long as the Minister of the Interior and the Governor of Istanbul remain in their posts, this investigation cannot be carried out properly. If they want it carried out properly, let them be removed from their duties. Let them either resign of their own accord, or let the Government remove them from office.

This brute force, the brute force in Taksim, attacked everyone but itself. It pinned something like a small red flag to its own lapel, so as to recognize one another..

OSMAN SARAÇ (Tokat) — The Turkish Flag, not something like a flag — the flag itself.

SADUN AREN (continuing) — And it attacked everyone not of its own kind, everyone without a flag on their chest. This is what this brute force is.

Süleyman Demirel
Prime Minister at the Time · TBMM, 17 February 1969

After that, we come to the beginning of these events. These events begin with a red flag being raised on the Beyazıt Tower. For six days now, in the country — in Istanbul especially — there has been an unending stream of marches, an unending stream of rallies, unending goings-on..

Fine, we say nothing against these — but one must think: how much tension are these creating in the country.

(...)

What did the police do in Taksim Square? They set up a cordon, cleared the square. Those who came to the square, those with a legal right to speak there under the law, would speak. That is how all of this has been explained... In the end, a scuffle broke out, and this scuffle unfortunately ended in a distressing way. But why should all this be hung around the government's neck? Consider the atmosphere, consider the state the citizen's nerves are in. How do you know the citizen won't think, "If occupying land is allowed, then other kinds of lawbreaking are allowed too"?

Millet Meclisi Tutanak Dergisi, Period 2, Volume 33, Session 4, 17 February 1969, pp. 475–476. — TBMM ↗

The group, marching legally, ignoring the police cordon, moved to follow the direction of the square toward Harbiye, all the while raising and lowering a banner reading "Sixth Fleet Get Out" in a provocative manner and making hand and arm gestures toward the crowd, and Molotov cocktails and stones began to be thrown. In the face of this unexpected conduct on the part of the marching group, individuals belonging to the opposing group, gathered here and there within the larger crowd, broke through the police cordon at one or two points in a sudden fit of rage, and responded to those marching legally with the stones, clubs, and various implements that had appeared.

(…) Events carrying within them various problems and the accumulated weight of provocations that had continued for years were brought to a close — thanks solely to the devoted efforts of the security forces — with as few casualties as possible.

22 February 1969, Bugün

Let us first note this: it was, above all, the provocations of the leftist press that brought the Taksim events to such a bloody point. Indeed, the deliberately staged photographs and news items published in these newspapers after the event once again gave away the true culprits.

Ufuk Şehri
İÜ TB Chairman · February 1969

In recent days, developments that shake the very foundations of the state have unsettled every reasonable and patriotic person. It is quite plain that the communists' daily attempts at some new move have finally caused the people to fly into a rage and attack. The true provocateurs behind the street clash that caused two deaths are the communists. Those who stage a street action every single day knew, and wanted, that such a reaction would come, and that the blood of brothers would be spilled.

Those who assess even our national struggle in class terms, calling it a "bourgeois revolutionary" affair — if these are not communists, then who is a communist in Turkey? Those who label rightists' call to jihad as the resurrection of the caliphate are themselves trying, under the name "call to a second war of freedom," to exploit Atatürkist youth for a "Red Jihad."

Turkish youth condemns every kind of provocation of enmity liable to cause a clash between brothers. The so-called "youth rally" is far from representative of Turkish youth. The Marxist group that dared to raise the red flag, through the exploitation of workers, is attempting, with a skillful maneuver, to pin these actions on the whole of Turkish youth.

Our fellow university students, who wage the intellectual struggle within the democratic order, must remain neutral and cautious in the face of any invitation to clash, from right or left alike. Genuine Turkish youth is the advocate and accomplice of no bloc outside its national interests. And the handful of rabble and children who joined the recent red marches are incapable of representing this youth. Genuine Turkish youth is dignified, noble, and faithful to Atatürk's principles. Those who fill the streets every day for nothing — thoughtless, degenerate lackeys of the Muscovite who unsettle the nation and the people — can never, and must never, represent Turkish youth.

I call on Atatürkist, nationalist youth to unite against these red slaves of imperialism.

Necip Fazıl Kısakürek
Büyük Doğu · May 1969

The University: In our universities, crumbling on every side and having already lost the power to remain standing, though this diagnosis is shared by right-wing and left-wing groups alike, it is more often than not the idealists who find themselves obliged to defend this sick institution against the materialists. Consider that sorrowful obligation: spiritually minded, traditionalist youth find themselves under a duty to protect an institution that admits of no defense — one that, in the future, will itself be their own target — from a leftist assault. When the lion sees a hyena pouncing on the prey it means to seize, it will naturally turn against the hyena, rather than stoop to the disgrace of sharing its prey with it. The field cannot be left to the crows.

The Left's Conduct: The same contradiction found in the university question — traditionalist youth being placed under an obligation to defend an institution they neither love nor find compatible with their own spirit — manifests itself, in exactly the same way, in the leftist camp's stance of siding with the American. Otherwise, no Turk who has downed a couple of whiskies and then torn up the Turkish Flag, and who thinks of nothing beyond a harsh policy of imperialism, could ever be loved by any Turk. But once the provocation of communism enters the picture, the matter changes character entirely. Today, America stands in the position of a D.D.T. against the Muscovite world. D.D.T. is used to be sprayed on insects, not drunk like strawberry syrup… And naturally, there is no such tendency as finding D.D.T.'s taste pleasant and coming to love it. To attempt to chase the American away with "Go home, get lost!" is to invite in the Muscovite and communism, and to break the D.D.T. bottle for the sake of the germs. It is the people and the youth, sensing this subtlety at a deep level, who showed their finest response to the left's conduct in the Taksim incident. To identify the true spirit of the incident, let us note this: what was effective in this matter was neither the M.T.T.B., nor the A.P. Youth Branch, nor the commandos, nor this, nor that! It was the people themselves, directly — the traditionalist masses… The day has now come to acknowledge that a people's and a nation's front has begun to establish itself on Turkish soil, and that it is this front that protects itself, its government, and its national policy! What fell to the government after the Taksim incident was to declare the following:

"You see that in Turkey, the nation is beginning to become a nation! Even if, as a government, we do not condone the incident and the unlawful acts, we must point out that the people, whose conscience-engine has now begun to run, have, after half a century of slumber and disorder, finally reached the point of proving their own existence — even if their vehicle strikes a few obstacles along the way, and even if they accept the penalty for this. We characterize this same people as having stepped onto the most fortunate of paths, under the warning to obey the laws, and we advise the traitors to the nation to open their eyes!"

The government's understanding did not run deep enough to deliver such a declaration, and the traditionalist Turkish people, as always, were once again left orphaned. But they can manage on their own.

Kısakürek, N.F. "Üniversite / Solun Davranışı" [The University / The Left's Conduct], Büyük Doğu, May 1969, No. 1. Quoted in: Kısakürek, N.F. (2009), Hadiselerin Muhasebesi 3, Istanbul: Büyük Doğu Yayınları, pp. 155–156. (The spelling errors in this piece are the author's own.)
Orhan Seyfi Orhon
Son Havadis · 20 February 1969

The Real Culprits

After the bloody incident in Taksim, wanting it to be even bloodier, every leftist newspaper opened a second front in their columns against us. They accuse the AP. They accuse the Prime Minister. They accuse the Minister of the Interior. They accuse the Governor of Istanbul, even the police force…

We are deeply saddened by our fallen brothers. But the organizers of these demonstrations did this knowing full well what would happen. Has this not been the case in previous demonstrations as well? It will be so in the ones to come, too.

Every leftist newspaper is attacking us because blood was spilled in Taksim. It is they who prepared the ground for the spilling of blood. Did they issue the slightest warning to the leftists? Among the blood spilled were our own innocent victims too.

Our fellow photojournalist Bahattin Şenol has only just come out of a coma. The son of our esteemed editor-in-chief was hospitalized after being wounded in the head.

Read the leftist newspapers! Our spilled blood is of no account to them! Not even worth mentioning. Have you seen it, in even a single one of them? Poor Şenol, lying dazed in a hospital bed? For the leftists, their own blood is precious — but it is they who prepared the ground for this catastrophe.

To listen to the leftist writers, you'd think these people had gone to Taksim for a festival. That, while singing national songs, they were set upon by enemies of the Turk! Whereas in reality, they went to Taksim to bring anarchy to Turkey, to bring communism to Turkey. The commotion they raised is too repugnant for the national conscience ever to accept. They attack the Sixth Fleet, calling it Turkey's ally. This Fleet is ours, part of our defense force. Does one attack the naval forces of a guest ally in one's own nation's harbor?

No nation's conscience could accept this. And what manner of attack, so utterly outside the bounds of national decency. Let me repeat, if you will pardon me, one of the placards they carried: "American Bastard. Beat It, Sixth Fleet!"

They say they were assaulted by religious zealots. It is they, again, who provoked using religion. Here, look, is the title of the joint declaration distributed by all the leftist unions: "To the American Infidel!"

One need not be a legal expert to understand this. Anyone can understand it. Put your hand on your conscience and decide: who deserves to hang. Please, tell me — in a gathering formed proudly, for this purpose, with this intent, would a fight not break out? Would there not be wounded, would there not be dead? We owe thanks to the Minister of the Interior, the Governor, the police, the government. They spared us this calamity to the extent that they did.

To the leftists and all their lackeys, I cry out, in the name of the Turkish nation, with the voice of the İstiklal Marşı: "You cannot bring the Bolshevik yoke upon this nation. You cannot enslave this people!"

Orhon, O.S. "Asıl Suçlular" [The Real Culprits], Son Havadis, 20 February 1969. Quoted in: Darendelioğlu, İ.E. (1995), Kanlı Pazar, Istanbul: Kamer, pp. 177–182.

Let Us Unite Against the American Infidel

Alongside this, the American infidel has begun bringing his Sixth Fleet into our territorial waters, using our harbors as a bed of prostitution for his sailors!.. While our unemployed wander the streets, our sick cannot find a hospital bed, and our children waste away for want of medicine, hotels are being closed off so that American sailors can engage in prostitution. The American Sixth Fleet's depraved commander, Admiral Charbonatt, dances the belly dance with a dancing girl's skirt tied around his waist, as if mocking our poverty!.. Our poor brothers in uniform stand guard for the Americans in the cold, so that they may do their jobs well!..

Our deceived Muslim brothers, having prayed against the infidel American Sixth Fleet, are being set upon our other Muslim brothers marching for independence!.. Muslims do not pray beneath infidel flags. Muslims fight until they drive out those flags. Jihad is not declared against brothers who demand independence, but against the plundering infidel, America!.. For our freedom, our bread, our honor, let us unite against the infidel America and its partners, let us fight.

"Amerikan Gavuruna Karşı Birleşelim" [Let Us Unite Against the American Infidel], Bağımsız Türkiye (FKF Bulletin), February 1969. Quoted in: Yıldırım, A. (2008), FKF Dev-Genç Tarihi, Istanbul: Doruk, pp. 616–617.
Yusuf Küpeli
FKF Chairman · Ant magazine, 4 March 1969

The anti-imperialist march the workers set in motion had, by the time it approached Taksim, grown — with the people joining in — into an independence army 30,000 strong. This independence army, whose aim was to explain the ugly wheel of exploitation in which our people found themselves caught, was naturally unarmed and had not formed up for combat. Even so, the anti-imperialist struggle that had begun in major cities such as İzmir, Ankara, Trabzon, Adana, and Gaziantep, and that was gradually coming to be embraced by the peasant masses, created a terrifying panic among America and its collaborators. This panic is what led them to organize the attack in Taksim. Those who organized it thought they could extinguish the anti-imperialist flame blazing in Taksim Square. For this reason, once only a small portion of the 30,000-strong crowd had entered the square, the rest were cut off by the police and left outside it. Stones were thrown at the independence fighters who entered the square, and they were attacked by police, brainwashed racist militants, and reactionary groups representing the intellectual plane of the feudal remnant. In this attack, pistols, low-trajectory fragmentation bombs, bottles, hatchets, axes, clubs, and truncheons were used. In carrying out this attack, the deceived — who had prayed against the Sixth Fleet and counted themselves Muslims — did not shrink from wounding to the point of death, and from killing, those they managed to seize. In the clashes that broke out in the side streets, the independence fighters, for their part, gave the sellouts and the deceived who fell into their hands the lesson they deserved.

In this affair, the press, too — controlled by finance capital — fell into the very same trap America applies everywhere. The events were presented as a simple clash between progressives and reactionaries, between the religious and the communist. It was never made clear that this was, in fact, a fight between our people, waging a war of independence, and the deceived forces sold out — in mind and in stomach — to imperialism. This is American imperialism's classic tactic. In the countries it exploits, it tries to set the people against one another by forming armies, among classes whose interests coincide with its own, in the service of its own interests. Before the march, many friends were assigned to bring it under control. Controlling the march was an important problem. To pile blame mercilessly on the security forces, on the city's governor, in every incident — to lay the whole of the responsibility on them by rote — is not a view we support. Consider the following:

Those emerging from the communal prayers held at the Dolmabahçe and Beşiktaş mosques were seen heading straight for Taksim Square, even before the march — organized in accordance with the law under the name "Workers' March Against Imperialism and Exploitation," announced to begin at Hürriyet Square and end in Taksim — had even begun. Osman Bölükbaşı
Millet Partisi Deputy · TBMM, 25 February 1969
About This Document

Osman Bölükbaşı is one of the important representatives of the nationalist camp in Turkish political history. He entered politics in 1946 through Adnan Menderes's Democrat Party, left the DP in 1947 to pursue a harder line of opposition against the CHP, took part in founding the Millet Partisi in 1948, and, in 1950, was elected as the Millet Partisi's sole deputy from his home province of Kırşehir. When his party was shut down in 1953 on the grounds that it "produced policy contrary to secularism," he founded the Cumhuriyetçi Millet Partisi in 1954 and was elected its chairman; when he was again elected as a deputy from Kırşehir in the 1954 elections, the DP government responded by demoting Kırşehir from a province to a district and attaching it to Nevşehir.

He became chairman of the Cumhuriyetçi Köylü Millet Partisi (CKMP), formed in 1958 through the merger of the CMP and the Türkiye Köylü Partisi; when the CKMP joined the coalition government formed by İnönü in 1962, he left the party together with 28 deputies and refounded the Millet Partisi. The CKMP, meanwhile, passed after Bölükbaşı to the leadership of a group that included Türkeş, who in 1969 renamed it the Nationalist Movement Party (MHP).

At the time of Kanlı Pazar, Osman Bölükbaşı was a Millet Partisi deputy. In a speech he gave in parliament days after Kanlı Pazar, on 24 February 1969, he accused the Justice Party government under Demirel of "stepping outside the bounds of legitimacy," of "seeking an ally in the street against the street," of "using masses that had enjoyed its tolerance and support, and that it had provoked, as a striking force," and of "turning our sacred mosques, through overt and covert incitement, organization, or toleration of certain individuals, into headquarters for political assault units."

Bölükbaşı's remarks underscore the assessment that Kanlı Pazar was a planned operation, and place responsibility for it directly on the Justice Party under Demirel's leadership.
