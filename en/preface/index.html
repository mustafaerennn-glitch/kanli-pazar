Preface

📎 Preface to the living edition May 2026

My interest in Kanlı Pazar — the 1969 Istanbul massacre — began as a student in the Department of Sociology at Mimar Sinan Fine Arts University. The undergraduate thesis I completed in 2008 was about eighty pages long. When the possibility of publishing it as a book came up in 2012, I revisited the text: I added new sources, spent long stretches tracking down material in secondhand bookshops, and combed the archives again. By the time it became a book, the work had grown to roughly two hundred pages.

In the years since, it has become clearer that Kanlı Pazar is not merely an event that belongs to the past. How it is remembered and retold, which of its facets are made visible and which fade over time, have become as much a part of the research as the event itself. New testimonies, television programs, documentaries, newspaper archives, pamphlets, photographs, and statements made years later all made it necessary to reopen this work.

For that reason, what you are reading is no longer just a republication of the 2012 book. It has been reconceived as an open-ended field of research — one that expands with new documents and testimonies, and that tries to make visible its own gaps, omissions, and contradictions. A printed book is finished at a given moment; a digital format, by contrast, allows the work to develop over time, to be transformed by new additions, and to be discussed anew.

Debates about Kanlı Pazar have too often been reduced to a clash between two opposing political camps. But the event should be considered within a broader historical context — one that stretches from the organizational forms of anticommunism in Turkey to the social legitimation of political violence, from the use of religious discourse for political mobilization to the polarization of youth movements. This work tries to bring that context into focus, and to trace, on a documentary basis, the relationships among the period's different actors.

The new material added to this digital edition is not merely "supplementary information." Some documents call into question narratives repeated for years; some testimonies require rethinking different facets of the event. In particular, self-critical statements made in later years open up new areas of debate about the political atmosphere of the period. For this reason, the work tries not only to record a past event, but also to trace how social memory is shaped, how it is transformed, and by what mechanisms it is reproduced.

I owe thanks to everyone who contributed along the way — with criticism, with documents, or with support for the work's development. The contributions of the teachers and friends I thanked in the earlier edition remain among the foundations of this work. The researchers, readers, and archive contributors who have joined in this new phase now hold an important place in its development as well.

Any shortcomings and errors in this work are entirely my own. But as I wrote in the preface years ago, every piece of research is also a road, and that road never fully ends. This edition should be understood as part of that ongoing search.

I dedicate this work to everyone working to dispel the fog that obscures the truth, and to ensure that suffering like this is never repeated.

Mustafa Eren

May 2026, Zurich

My interest in Kanlı Pazar — the 1969 Istanbul massacre — began as a student in the Department of Sociology at Mimar Sinan Fine Arts University. When I submitted the Kanlı Pazar study as my undergraduate thesis in 2008, it was only 80 pages. When the possibility of publishing it as a book came up in 2012, I went through it again. To that end, I added to my sources many studies that had appeared since 2008, along with books I was able to find in secondhand bookshops, and I carried out a fresh archive search. By the time the thesis had become a book, it ran to 200 pages.

One of the more demanding stages began after that. So that the work could pass through critical eyes, I sent it to my teachers and friends. I owe each of them my thanks. I would like to sincerely thank Sibel Yardımcı, my thesis advisor, who supported this work from the start and encouraged me to turn it into a book, reading everything I wrote with great care; Yıldırım Şentürk, who showed great understanding in allowing me to step back from our joint research project while I devoted time to the book; Berna Uçarol, Burak Onaran, Ebru Aykut, Erkan Karabay, Hülya Mete, Mesut Aydıner, Seçil Doğuç, Suna Yılmaz Açıkel, and Şükrü Aslan, who gave generously of their time and effort to review the manuscript before publication; and Tevrat Asyalı, who stood by me and never withheld support, from the archival research stage through to the writing itself. I also owe thanks to the Kalkedon family for their patience and attentiveness through the changes I made to the work.

Whatever merit this book has emerged from the contributions of my teachers and friends, who spared no effort. Its shortcomings are entirely my responsibility. Aware that every piece of research is a road, and that this road never ends, I hope to make up for these shortcomings with new work as I continue along it.

I dedicate this book to everyone striving to ensure that the same suffering is never repeated…

Mustafa Eren

January 2012, Istanbul
